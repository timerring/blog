Function g(x) as a R.V. #

Continuous case #

We can compute probabilities of events relating to Y=g(X) through probabilities of events concerning X

$$ \begin{align*} P(G \subseteq B) &= P(\omega \in \Omega: Y(\omega) \in B) = P(\omega \in \Omega: X(\omega) \in g^{-1}(B)) = P(X \in g^{-1}(B)) \end{align*} $$

$$ F_{Y}(y)=P(Y(\xi) \leq y)=P(g(X(\xi)) \leq y)=P\left(X(\xi) \leq g^{-1}(-\infty, y]\right) . $$

eg.

$$ y=a x+b $$

Solution: Suppose a>0

$$ F_{Y}(y)=P(Y(\xi) \leq y)=P(a X(\xi)+b \leq y)=P\left(X(\xi) \leq \frac{y-b}{a}\right)=F_{X}\left(\frac{y-b}{a}\right) . $$

$$ f_{Y}(y)=\frac{1}{a} f_{x}\left(\frac{y-b}{a}\right) . $$

Else if a<0 then

$$ F_{Y}(y)=P(Y(\xi) \leq y)=P(a X(\xi)+b \leq y)=P\left(X(\xi)>\frac{y-b}{a}\right) =1-F_{x}\left(\frac{y-b}{a}\right), $$

$$ f_{Y}(y)=-\frac{1}{a} f_{x}\left(\frac{y-b}{a}\right) . $$

In general,

$$ f_{Y}(y)=\frac{1}{|a|} f_{X}\left(\frac{y-b}{a}\right) . $$

eg2. $y=x^2$

$$ F_{Y}(y)=P(Y(\xi) \leq y)=P\left(X^{2}(\xi) \leq y\right) . $$

If y<0 then the event $({X^{2}(\xi) ≤y}=\phi,)$ and hence $F_{Y}(y)=0, y<0 .$

For (y>0) the event ${Y(\xi) ≤y}={X^{2}(\xi) ≤y}$ is equivalent to ${x_{1}\leq X(\xi) \leq x_{2}} .$

$$ F_{Y}(y)=P\left(x_{1}<X(\xi) \leq x_{2}\right)=F_{X}\left(x_{2}\right)-F_{X}\left(x_{1}\right) =F_{X}(\sqrt{y})-F_{X}(-\sqrt{y}), y>0 . $$

By direct differentiation, we get

Some useful skills:

eg. $x_{1}=1 / y$

Here for every y, $x_{1}=1 / y$ is the only solution, and

$\frac{d y}{d x}=-\frac{1}{x^{2}}$ so that $|\frac{dy}{dx}_{x=x_1}|=\frac{1}{1 / y^{2}}=y^{2}$

we obtain $f_{Y}(y)=\frac{1}{y^{2}} f_{X}(\frac{1}{y})$

Discrete case #

Suppose X is a discrete-type r.v with $P(X=x_{i})=p_{i}$ , $x=x_{1}, x_{2}, \cdots, x_{i}, \cdots$ and $Y=g(X)$

Clearly Y is also of discrete-type, and when $(x=x_{i})$ , $(y_{i}=g(x_{i})$ and for those $(y_{i})$

$$ P\left(Y=y_{1}\right)=P\left(X-x_{1}\right)=p_{p} y=Y_{1}, y_{2}, … $$

Mean & Variance #

Mean #

Mean: the average （mean） value of the r.v. in a very large number of trials.

$$ E(Y)=E(g(X))=\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} y f_{Y}(y) d y=\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} g(x) f_{X}(x) d x $$

Variance #

$$ \sigma_{x}^{2}=E\left[(X-\mu)^{2}\right]>0 $$

With g(X) = (X-μ)^2, we have

$$ \sigma_{x}^{2}=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(x - \mu)^{2}f_{X}(x)dx,t>0 $$

Besides,

$$ Var(X)=E(X^{2})-\mu^{2} $$

Moments #

The moments of X is defined as

$$ E(X^{n}) $$

The central moments of X is defined as

$$ E\left[(X-\mu)^{n}\right] $$

Characteristic Functions #

The characteristic function of a r.v X is defined as

$$ \Phi_{X}(\omega)=E(e^{jX\omega})=\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty}e^{j\omega x}f_{X}(x)dx $$

$$ \Phi_{X}(\omega)=\sum_{k}e^{j\omega k}P(X = k) $$

Thus $\Phi_{X}(0) = 1$, and $|\Phi_{X}(\omega)|\leq1$ for all $\omega$. $\to$ MAX at 0

Useful when in derivation.

Chebychev Inequality #

$$ P(|X - \mu|\geq\epsilon)\geq\frac{\sigma^{2}}{\epsilon^{2}} $$

In particular with $\epsilon = k\sigma$ we have

$$ P(|X - \mu|\geq k\sigma)\leq\frac{1}{k^{2}} $$

eg k = 2, so inside the range will be at least 1 - 1/4 = 3/4

Compared with gaussian, the chebychev inequality is not tight. it indicates the upperbound which means worst case.
